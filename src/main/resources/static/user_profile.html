Learn About Blood Donation

Why Blood Donation Matters

Every 2 seconds, someone in the world needs blood.
Blood cannot be manufactured, it can only come from
generous donors.
One donation can save up to 3 lives.

Who Can Donate?

Age: 18–65 years (varies by country regulations).
Weight: At least 50 kg (110 lbs).
Must be in good general health.
No recent major illnesses, infections, or risky behaviors.


Who Cannot Donate?

People with certain chronic illnesses (e.g., uncontrolled diabetes, heart disease).
Recent surgery, pregnancy, or blood transfusion.
Travel to malaria-affected regions (temporary deferral).
Low hemoglobin levels.

Donation Process – Simple & Safe

Health Check – Quick questionnaire and mini-physical.
Donation – Takes about 8–10 minutes.
Rest & Refresh – Free snacks, water, and rest to re-energize.
(The entire process usually takes 30–45 minutes.)

Myths vs Facts

✘ “Donating blood makes you weak.”
✔ Your body replaces the lost blood within 24–48 hours.

✘ “Blood donation is painful.”
✔ Only a slight pinch, like a regular injection.

✘ “Only older people need blood.”
✔ Accident victims, newborns, cancer patients, and mothers in childbirth often need it.

Benefits of Donating Blood

Saves lives and supports community healthcare.
Free health screening (blood pressure, hemoglobin, infections).
Reduces harmful iron levels in frequent male donors.
Gives a sense of purpose and contribution.
